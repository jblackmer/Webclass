Anxiety of My Own Making

Anxiety of My Own Making

I picture myself on a golden ship
Ignore the screeching child to my left.

I dare to risk a jump into the unknown
Wifi is the enemy of timely notifications.

With only a coin in hand
Brightness up or the ticket won't scan.

Waiting for the gods to be kind
MTA don't let me down.

Preparing for them to lie
Wrong seat = motion sickness

I can only hope
Will my suitcase fall? Hit my head?

To reach my destination
Please don't delay again.

Colorful (Warning: Language)

Dear Two-Faced Bitch,

I am restrained
By some fool's desire
To put your needs
Above my own.

I
Am
Done.

I'm tired.
Sleeping,
Waking asleep.
And I want to be angry
But your tears would hurt
Some little part inside
That still cares.

That feels like bullshit now.

Congratulations
On your brave act
My knight
In shining glass armor.
I hope it burns
Melds into your skin.

Remind yourself to cry,
You did for me.

I would be the bigger person
And I find that easy to do
Looking down at you
In the lowest pit of hell.

I want to understand
But I don't.
I'll never understand
Your need to hurt me
To patch yourself up.

I think I need to become a superhero.
Not to save you
From the hole you dug yourself into
But to read minds.
That's what you wanted
Right?

Don't worry
You aren't the villain.
The criteria needs you
To be more than an asshole.

I would throw a drink at you
If that weren't a waste of $4.38.

I would scream
But I'd rather lose my voice
Singing karaoke on a late night.

I would do so many things if you looked at me
But I don't have it in me to care anymore.

So take your self-serving apologies
And your masked well-wishes
To someone who still
Would work to repair the damages.
I won't be that person.

With all the respect you earned,
Fuck you.

Dividing Sunlight for an Orange Tree

Dividing Sunlight for an Orange Tree

Oranges glow in the first
Rays of sunlight
That hit the morning dew
At an angle where
The colors wake with
A gentle greeting.

Oranges whisper
In tongues.
Rustling leaves
Sharing sweet secrets
Only they have the privilege
To know.

Oranges sway
In the warm embrace of the sun
Scattering its rays
To reach every nook,
Every corner
Of the orange tree.

Oranges fall,
Their nutrients
Squishing into the coolness
Of the soil
So that the light may
Reach the newborn oranges.

I Hope You Feel Better

I Hope You Feel Better

Snatching the space
In my lungs
For your own breath.


Go on ,
build your fortress.
Throw the first stone
Round and smooth and shiny
Made with care
With glass, salt and steel.


For my fool's errand
I wait for the first tear
Into my flesh,
Hoping you
Care.


The blood is spilling
So violently red,
So viciously blue,
Thank you for checking
On this broken machine.
I still cry.


This machine
Will gaze at you
In your glass armor.
Watch you throw your next stone.
You don't look
But I beg you to shatter.


I mean the best but wish the worst.

Shadow Waltz

Shadow Waltz

Remember
The waltz dance,
Where thorns gnash,
Blood petals.

“The waltz. Dance.”
She alone
Bleeds petals
Into ruin.

She, alone
Leads the dance
Into ruin.
To its end.

Lead the dance.
Know the steps.
To its end,
Keep the mask.

Know the steps
Are slippery.
Keep the mask
From falling.


It's slippery.
Be careful
Of falling.
Almost done.

“Be careful,”
She whispers.
“Almost done.”
Whispers back.


She whispers,
The ballroom
Whispers back,
“Remember.”

While You Were Gone

While You Were Gone

Return to the sea
Right where you left me
With my toes buried in the sand
Waiting for the gentle caress
Of the water
The sand burns.

So new
So vile
I forget the moments I once laughed
At the eager salt in the night air
Beckoning me back
To the water.

Give me the rain to cry.

Gentle lapping of the sea
To the screams of a hurricane.
My quiet days are the loudest.
Waves erode
My stone shell
Return me to the water

Swim against my skin, remind me I'm alive.

Seaweed, wrap my soul
In a swift embrace
And drag me into the depths
Where I can hope to rise
Pour my soul
From the sky
Wash away my pain
From the sandy footprints
To the old wooden porch
So I know I can move forward
Away from the water.

We shall emerge from the sea, you and I.
